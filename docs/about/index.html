About IdeaPitch

open source ↗

An open platform where anyone can post an idea for something that doesn't exist yet — an app, a website, a tool, a library, a CLI, or anything digital.

Why IdeaPitch?
💡

For everyone

Sometimes you have a new idea — something you believe should exist but doesn't. Or maybe you came across something in your daily use and wondered why there's no app, tool, or library to make it easier. IdeaPitch is where you post it, and where builders find it.

🔨

For developers

Looking for your next project but can't find an idea worth building? Every idea here is something someone genuinely needed and couldn't find.

How it works
1

Post your idea

Describe in up to 500 characters the thing you wish existed. Be specific. You can always add more details in the comments.

2

Community votes

Others upvote ideas they want too. The most requested rise to the top.

Rules
⚠️

IdeaPitch is not a search engine. It's for unique ideas that genuinely don't exist yet. This is your last resort — after you've searched and found nothing. Also check the Closed section before posting. Make sure what you're posting doesn't already exist.

  • Search before posting — check the Closed section too. If it already exists, don't post it.
  • Be specific — a clear idea is easier to build. You can add more details in the comments if 500 characters aren't enough.
  • One idea per post.
  • No links in the idea itself. If you need to reference something, leave the link in the comments.
  • Keep comments relevant to the idea — off-topic comments are not allowed.
  • Be respectful in comments.
  • Anyone can comment on an idea — to let the poster know it already exists, to discuss details, to say you're building it as a developer, or to share a link to a matching project.
  • If your idea gets built or someone informs you it already exists, please close it on GitHub. Moderators will also close it when verified.
